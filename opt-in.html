SMS Opt-In

How Kaku collects consent before sending you text messages.

How it works

Kaku is an AI-powered group event coordinator. When someone you know uses Kaku to plan an event and adds you as a participant, here's what happens:

1

You receive an introductory message

Kaku sends you a single text message identifying who added you, what the event is about, and asks for your consent to continue messaging.

"Hi! Your friend Justin is using Kaku to plan brunch this weekend and added you. Reply YES to participate and receive event updates, or reply STOP to opt out. Msg & data rates may apply."

2

You reply YES to consent

Only after you reply YES does Kaku begin sending you event coordination messages, such as availability questions, confirmations, and updates.

3

You can opt out at any time

Reply STOP to any message to immediately stop receiving texts from Kaku. Reply HELP for support.

Important information

  • Messages are sent for event coordination only — never for marketing or promotional purposes.
  • Message frequency varies based on event activity (typically 2–10 messages per event).
  • Message and data rates may apply.
  • Consent is not a condition of any purchase.
  • Reply STOP to unsubscribe at any time. Reply HELP for help.
  • For support, contact support@kaku-ai.com.
